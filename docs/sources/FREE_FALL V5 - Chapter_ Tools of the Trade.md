# **Chapter: Tools of the Trade (V5 Draft)**

In the fractured future of 2048, capability is often bought, installed, or equipped. The tools you carry, the armor you wear, the weapons you wield, the augmentations fused to your body, and the machines you pilot are not just accessories – they are extensions of your will and the primary source of your effectiveness. This chapter details the various types of equipment available to mercenaries operating on the edge, from personal gear and augmentations to powerful vehicles and exoskeletons.

Success often hinges on having the right tool for the job ("Having the right tool IS the skill") and understanding its capabilities and costs, particularly the constant strain it places on your core Attributes through Binding.

## **General Gear Concepts & Qualities**

Many pieces of equipment share common rules and qualities that define their function and interaction with the core mechanics.

**(V4 to V5 Change):** The core concepts remain similar, but V5 places more emphasis on the *rationale* behind Attribute Binding and clarifies the function of qualities like Hardened, especially for vehicles. The V5 template standardizes how items are presented.

**Attribute Binding (Attribute X):**

* **Concept:** Significant Gear/Augmentations bind X points of a specified Character Attribute (Body, Mind, or Ghost), reducing the maximum Current points available (Permanent Attribute \- Total Binding Cost \= Max Current Attribute). This represents the constant physical strain, power draw, neurological load, cognitive effort, or existential toll the item demands. This embodies the "Capability has Costs" principle.  
* **Persistence:** The Binding Cost remains even if the gear becomes Faulty or Broken, persisting until it is properly removed, uninstalled (often requiring downtime/surgery for augmentations), or discarded.  
* **Cumulation:** Binding costs from multiple items are cumulative. Managing the total Binding Cost across your Attributes is crucial.  
* **Rationale (V5 Emphasis):**  
  * **Body Binding:** Typically represents physical weight, bulk, power requirements drawing on biological energy, or the strain of physically demanding gear/augmentations (e.g., heavy armor, powered exoskeletons, demanding biological mods).  
  * **Mind Binding:** Primarily represents the cognitive load required to process the gear's input/output, manage its systems, handle complex interfaces, or maintain focus while using demanding tech (e.g., sensor suites, complex weapon systems, cyber warfare tools). It's the mental bandwidth consumed.  
  * **Ghost Binding:** Represents the strain on the user's social adaptability, composure, willpower, predictive capabilities, or the mental effort needed to manage intrusive social-stealth tech, deep neural interfaces, or complex networked systems. It often reflects the cost of integrating intrusive tech, suppressing/altering baseline human responses, or the existential weight of replacing humanity with machinery (especially common with advanced Cybernetics).

**Armor Value (AV X / AV X vs \[Type\]):**

* **Function:** Provides X points of protection against Harm. Default is Physical unless specified (e.g., AV 1 vs Environmental). Reduces incoming Harm levels before resolution.  
* **Minimum Harm:** AV never reduces Harm suffered below **1 level**, unless an attack or effect specifically states otherwise. Weapons with the **High Impact** quality inflict a minimum of **2 levels** of Harm, regardless of AV.  
* **(V4 to V5 Change):** Functionally identical to V4.

**Hardened (Gear):**

* **Function:** This gear can absorb more punishment.  
  * The first time you choose **Option C: Break Bound Gear** involving this item to negate a level of Harm, it becomes **Faulty** instead of Broken.  
  * A **Faulty** item ceases to function, but its Attribute Binding Cost remains. The Faulty condition is temporary and is automatically repaired during the next Regroup or Downtime period.  
  * If you choose **Option C: Break Bound Gear** involving this item while it is already **Faulty**, it becomes truly **Broken** (requires repair).  
* **(V4 to V5 Change):** Functionally identical to V4 for personal gear. (Vehicle/Exo Hardened functions differently in V5 \- see Vehicle section).

**Integrated System (\[System Name\]):**

* **Function:** The gear includes a built-in system (e.g., Integrated Comms, Basic Medkit). May add Binding Cost or have specific usage requirements.  
* **(V4 to V5 Change):** Functionally identical to V4.

**Limited Use:**

* **Function:** Represents a finite resource. When making an action check using this item, if **any d20 rolled shows a '1'**, the item is considered fully expended after the action resolves (complications, breakage, resource depletion). Cannot be used again until replenished/replaced.  
* **(V4 to V5 Change):** Functionally identical to V4.

**Load Bearing (X \[Attribute/Any\]):**

* **Function:** Helps distribute weight/power for attached equipment. Ignores up to X total points of Attribute Binding cost from weapons or other gear attached directly to this item. Can specify Attribute type (Body, Mind, Ghost) or 'Any'. Applies even when attached gear is actively used.  
* **(V4 to V5 Change):** Functionally identical to V4.

## **Augmentations: More Than Human**

**(V4 to V5 Change):** This section was separate in V4 but is integrated here in V5. The core concept of augmentations providing capability at the cost of Binding remains central. The main changes are the **category names and descriptions** (Biological/Biomechanical \-\> Spliced/Bionic) and a stronger emphasis on **Ghost Binding** reflecting the transhuman cost, especially for Cybernetics. The V5 template is used for examples.

In the unforgiving environments of 2048, the baseline human form is often insufficient. Augmentations – modifications to the body and mind – are not just advantages; they are often necessities, blurring the line between operator and equipment. They enhance capabilities, grant new senses, and are frequently the only way to overcome the lasting damage of **Permanent Harm** by replacing or repairing what was lost. Like all advanced gear, augmentations grant significant capabilities but come at a cost, integrating directly into your being and taxing your core Attributes via **Attribute Binding**.

**Attribute Binding (Augmentations):** Functions identically to gear Binding Costs, representing the constant biological, neurological, or cognitive resources the augmentation consumes. The rationale (Body/Mind/Ghost) is crucial for reflecting the type of strain.

**Categories of Augmentation (V5):**

* **Spliced:**  
  * *Modifications rooted in genetic manipulation, tailored biology, or organic enhancements.*  
  * **Examples:** Gene therapies boosting muscle density, symbiotic microbes enhancing resilience, vat-grown organ replacements with superior function, glands secreting combat stimulants, bio-toxin sacs.  
  * **Typical Binding:** Often **Body** (metabolic cost, physical change) or **Mind** (altered biochemistry affecting focus/perception).  
  * **Nature:** Generally internal, modifying existing biology. Less commonly used for replacing limbs lost to Permanent Harm, but can replace damaged organs or enhance existing functions. (Replaces V4's "Biological").  
* **Bionic:**  
  * *Fusions of flesh and machine, integrating mechanical components with the biological form.*  
  * **Examples:** Articulated mechadendrites, reinforced skeletal structures laced with polymers, integrated tool systems built into limbs, optical sensors grafted alongside organic eyes, common replacements for limbs/senses lost to Permanent Harm.  
  * **Typical Binding:** Can bind **Body** (physical integration, power), **Mind** (control systems), or **Ghost** (sensory integration, neural links), depending on complexity and invasiveness.  
  * **Nature:** A hybrid approach, often involving both internal and external components. (Replaces V4's "Biomechanical").  
* **Cybernetic:**  
  * *Primarily technological replacements or additions integrated with the user's neurology.*  
  * **Examples:** Full limb or organ replacements with synthetic equivalents, dedicated data ports, advanced sensory suites replacing organic senses, complex neural interfaces providing direct machine control. A primary method for restoring function after severe Permanent Harm.  
  * **Typical Binding:** Most frequently bind **Ghost** or **Mind**, representing the significant neurological load, the processing demand, and the existential cost of replacing humanity with complex machinery. Can also bind **Body** for particularly demanding physical replacements (e.g., heavy integrated weapons, full torso replacements).  
  * **Nature:** Often involves significant replacement of organic parts with artificial systems, deeply integrated with the nervous system. (Consistent with V4's "Cybernetic", but with stronger emphasis on Ghost/Mind binding rationale).

**Integration: Invasive vs. Field-Operable:**

* **Invasive:** Require significant surgery/downtime for installation/removal (internal organs, neural interfaces, skeletal reinforcement, most limb/sensory replacements). Binding Cost is constant once installed.  
* **Field-Operable:** More flexible use (external modules, injectors, systems that can be activated/deactivated). Can often be swapped/attached/activated during mission prep or Regroup (GM discretion). Binding Cost only applies while active/attached.  
* **(V4 to V5 Change):** Functionally identical to V4.

## **Weapons**

**(V4 to V5 Change):** Weapon rules and qualities are largely consistent between V4 and V5. The main update is ensuring all entries use the V5 template and include a Binding rationale. The DV examples remain the same. Critical Hit rules are consistent.

Firearms, melee implements, and specialized ordnance are the most direct tools for applying force or neutralizing threats.

**Weapon Qualities:**

* **Ammo (\[Type\]):** Runs out on attack roll of '1'. Requires action/effort to reload. Types influence frequency/availability. (Consistent)  
* **Burst Fire:** Each Success \= 1 hit. First hits primary target, extras hit additional nearby declared targets or add hits to declared targets. (Consistent)  
* **Damage Value (DV):** Base Levels of Harm inflicted. (Consistent)  
* **High Impact:** Inflicts minimum 2 levels of Harm, regardless of AV. (Consistent)  
* **High Visibility:** Creates noticeable effect, gives away position. (Consistent)  
* **Piercing:** Ignores 1 level of target's AV. Can have higher values (e.g., Piercing 3 ignores 3 AV).  
* **Range (Close/Medium/Long):** Optimal distance. Attacking outside optimal range requires a natural 20 on at least one die to hit (inflicts normal DV, not doubled for crit). (Consistent)  
* **(New) Spread:** Each Success hits the primary target. Additional Successes can hit other targets within melee range of the primary target (max 1 hit per additional target).  
* **(New) Unwieldy:** Increases the Target Number (TN) by \+5 when attacking targets at Close range.

**Damage Values (DV) & Inflicting Harm:** (Consistent with V4)

| DV | Description | Example Harm Type | Example Weapons |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1 | Minor/Non-Lethal | Psychic/Physical | Shock Baton, Stun Grenade, Tranquilizer |
| 2 | Standard | Physical | Sidearms, Assault Rifles, SMGs, Plasma Rifle |
| 3 | Heavy | Physical | Sniper Rifles, Heavy Pistols, Shotguns, Ram |
| 4+ | Devastating | Physical | Explosives, Anti-Materiel Rifles, Rail Guns |

**Inflicting Harm:** (Consistent with V4)

* **Standard Hit:** Inflicts weapon's DV in Levels of Harm (after AV, min 1 Harm / 2 for High Impact).  
* **Critical Hit (Natural 20):**  
  * *Within Optimal Range:* Inflicts double DV (before AV, min 1 Harm / 2 for High Impact).  
  * *Outside Optimal Range:* Required to hit at all; inflicts normal DV.

## **Vehicles & Exoskeletons (Exos)**

**(V4 to V5 Change):** The core mechanics (Machine Attributes FRM/SYS, Attribute Substitution, Pilot Binding, Pilot Choice for Harm) remain consistent with the V4 descriptions provided. The main V5 refinement is the specific function of the **Hardened** quality for vehicles/exos, which now includes a **Faulty** state with operational penalties rather than just delaying Broken status. Examples updated to V5 template.

Complex machines offering enhanced mobility, protection, and firepower. They operate under specific rules modifying the core system.

**Core Concepts:**

* **Machine Attributes:** Vehicles/Exos have **Frame (FRM)** and **Systems (SYS)** attributes (Permanent/Current values), representing physical integrity/motive power and onboard computers/sensors, respectively.  
* **Attribute Substitution:** When piloting, use machine FRM/SYS instead of pilot Body/Mind for relevant actions/resistance involving the machine. Pilot's own attributes used for internal state checks.  
* **Pilot Binding Cost:** Operating the machine imposes a Binding Cost on the pilot's Attributes (Body, Mind, or Ghost) for the interface/control strain, separate from machine attributes. Integrated systems might add Binding; Hardpoint gear does not.  
* **Vehicle/Exo Durability & Harm:**  
  * **AV:** Machines have their own AV.  
  * **Attribute Damage:** Harm targeting the machine (after AV) reduces Current FRM (Physical) or SYS (Psychic/System), if the pilot allocates Harm to the machine.  
  * **Spending Machine Attributes:** Pilot can spend 1 Current FRM/SYS instead of Body/Mind to negate 1 level of Harm targeting the machine or meet an action prerequisite involving the machine. Cannot substitute for Ghost.  
  * **Pilot Choice for Harm:** For each level of Harm targeting the machine (after AV), pilot chooses:  
    1. **Allocate to Machine:** Spend 1 Current FRM/SYS (if available).  
    2. **Allocate to Pilot:** Pilot suffers the Harm level (uses own Harm slots/mitigation).  
  * **Becoming Broken:** If machine cannot absorb Harm (attribute 0\) and pilot doesn't take it, the machine becomes **Broken** (non-functional, pilot Binding remains). Functions like Harm Option C for the machine.  
  * **Pilot Safety:** Generally safe unless attack bypasses machine, as long as Harm can be allocated to FRM/SYS.

**Vehicle & Exo Qualities (V5):**

* **Cargo Capacity (X):** Cargo space. (Consistent)  
* **Hardened (Vehicle/Exo \- V5 Specific):** Functions differently than gear Hardened.  
  * The first time the machine would become **Broken**, it becomes **Faulty** instead (suffers significant operational penalties \- e.g., reduced speed/sensor range/weapon accuracy, GM discretion \- but remains minimally functional). Binding Cost remains.  
  * If it would become **Broken** again while **Faulty**, it becomes truly **Broken** (non-functional).  
* **Hardpoints (X \[Type\]):** Mount points for integrated weapons (Light, Heavy, Support, etc.). Mounted weapons don't add Pilot Binding. Firing checks use SYS (targeting) or FRM (exo melee). (Consistent)  
* **Integrated System (\[System Name\]):** Built-in functions (Advanced Sensors, ECM, etc.). May add Pilot Binding or require SYS/FRM checks. (Consistent)  
* **Movement System (\[Type/Rating\]):** Defines movement (EVA Thrusters, Space Drive, Walker Legs). Modifies/clarifies FRM use for movement. (Consistent)  
* **Occupancy (X):** Pilot \+ Passengers. (Consistent)  
* **Remote Operation:** Can be controlled remotely (potential penalties, requires specific gear). Checks likely use operator Mind/Ghost. (Consistent)  
* **Sensor Suite (\[Type/Rating\]):** Onboard sensor capabilities. Checks use SYS. (Consistent)  
* **Size Category (\[Category\]):** Abstract size (Personal, Small, Medium, Large, Huge). (Consistent)
